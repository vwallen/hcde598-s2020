Assignment 4: I like Lamp, Part I

Sketches

First round of ideas was very octopus focused, because they're great.

We have empty spots on our walls where sconces used to go, so I conceived of a pair of sconces that shaped like octopus bodies, which cast tentacular shadows.

Next ideas: floor lamps, shaped like floating octopus, jellyfish, or man o' war... with an internal, curved support rod holding them up without appearing to.

The floor lamp idea inspired an idea of small lamps, at floor level the could be scattered in a room and project shapes on the floor. The aquatic theme continued via diatom shapes which are animal and crystaline all at once.

In an effort to simplify, the diatoms became Platonic solids, which are cool nerdy shapes. Just top notch solids, A+.

I don't really know how, but at some point, they gained glowing projections and the most ready source of translucent cylinders that I have are Talenti jars.

How to treat the jars to play with light triggered some other ideas: by linking them in a closed system, liquid or air could be circulated to create swirls or vortexes of particles inside. A system the forced air could turn tubines in each jar for motion.

Prototypes

The first round of prototyping was based on creating shapes that were compatible with the jars, and could be combined later with other designs.

A pair of YouTube tutorials about creating threaded nuts and bolts was key to be able to make these. My initial prototype used a subtractive approach for the "lid" as in the video, but this version used the rail sweep approach for both pieces.

The second round was to create panels for producing one of the solids using the jar shapes.

These panels are thin and designed to be assembled using small cable ties. The lamp is attached by virtue of the bulb being too big to fit through the hole.

I had some bed adhesion issues printing something this thin and flat at this size, so that's going to be a factor moving forward.

I used tracing paper in the jars to prototype different frosting finishes or jar contents.

The prototype did receive some inspection.
