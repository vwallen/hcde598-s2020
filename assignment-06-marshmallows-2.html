Assignment 6: Marshmallow Paws

Design

Process

After a few false starts with modelling an paw shape that balanced realism, practicality, and adorableness, I ended with a shape based on lofting curves and cage editing, in a "bar-like" form. For a future iteration I would like to explore scultping tools to create a more organic shape.

Using this shape, I build my mold around it. First creating a rectangular solid to enclose it, and then using boolean operations to create the mold itself.

My sprue is a large conical shape, with a plug designed to hold the stick in place while the casting material sets. I used three pairs of ellipsoid keys, space along the long edge of the mold. The seam is placed along the read surface of the bar, where it will be less noticeable.

With my mold design complete, I created my mold-mold using another rectangular solid and boolean operations.

Because the mold join would be vertically aligned during set-up, I also made clamps to hold the mold together with rubber bands, designed to distribute the tension evenly.
